Universal Creative Tools GUI

Designing for Fellow Peers

ROLE

UX/UI Design, Interaction Design

TEAM

1-5 people

BRIEF

Produce an internal tool for real-time refining, fine-tuning, user test and adjust of interactive experiences, attractions, and games.

YEAR

2018, 2020


Confidentiality Notice: I am unable to disclose certain aspects of this project as it serves many products that are still under development.


STREAMLINE DESIGN & DEVELOPMENT

Several Universal Creative projects require an ample amount of test and adjust to tune experiences for our varied theme park guests. Rather than hard-coding changes during development, I teamed up with a fellow co-worker to produce internal tools, a GUI, to allow for real-time test and adjust of several larger interactive experiences.


DESIGN

I identified the needs of the project(s) and organized the information into groups of features with usability in mind. I created wireframes, collaborated with project peers to iterate on the layouts and streamline the user experience. I created mockups based on the wireframes and eventually exported individual assets for the developer to implement. The mockups also defined the microinteractions involved, if any. I enjoyed theming the tool(s) to the particular intellectual property of the project so it can also be used during formal demonstrations to stakeholders.

My final deliveries included documentation and a sprite sheet or blank feature assets that the ongoing developers could use if a new ask came up after my initial work was completed.



Designing for your peers can be very rewarding. It can lead to create better overall products.



MADE FOR ONE, USABLE BY ALL

The end product delivered to Project Team(s) is a GUI linked to the software driving different aspects of the attraction or experience such as graphics, lighting, gaming parameters and more. This helped especially with A/B testing purposes.


We discovered early into development that this tool could be used by a breadth of our peers beyond just the attraction designers, so usability was key. The tools could also be used by vendors and potentially park operations after the project goes live. As the tool leaves our hands, tool training could potentially be lost. Thus, the tool needed to be intuitive so that it can be effective even with little knowledge on its operation.
