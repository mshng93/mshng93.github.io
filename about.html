Background

Once upon a time, I had a tough time deciding the direction I would pursue for university. I was torn between my creative craftsy personality and the scientific process-oriented studies I excelled in as an I.B. student. I was then introduced to the Interactive Arts and Technology major at Simon Fraser University where I discovered UX which allowed me to practice both!


Professional Development

I really enjoyed the people-focused aspects of UX where I got to engage with others, learn about their stories, and how my products could help benefit them. To expand my world of UX, I participated in several student groups such as taking on lead roles in my University's UNICEF chapter as well as my faculty's student union where I co-founded a charitable program called 'Design For Donations'. My work led me to some freelance opportunities and eventually my internship with Universal Orlando Resort.


UX in Theme Parks

I landed an internship with the Advanced Technology Interactives Group at Universal Orlando Resort and was eventually hired on full-time as a designer. I got the opportunity to design for a very diverse audience on a variety of mediums including mobile, web, TV, and custom digital interfaces. I enjoyed solving challenges with my Developer partners-in-crime and other cross-functional teams as we strategized how to create the best products that told the stories of the intellectual properties we worked with.
