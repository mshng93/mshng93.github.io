Creole Carnival Games

A Mobile Treasure Hunt Around the Parks

ROLE

Mobile App Product Owner, Product Design

TEAM

5-10 people

LOCATION

Orlando, FL

Brief

Create a live guest-facing mobile app product for the Universal Orlando Resort™ Mardi Gras 25th anniversary event. The product was sponsored by our corporate partner The Coca-Cola® Company.

EVENT PERIOD

Feb. 1 - Apr. 2, 2020

The Challenge

2020 marked the 25th anniversary of Universal Orlando's Mardi Gras event. There was a desire to add more content to the festivites - one of which was the 'Creole Carnival Games' mobile experience. However, the initial idea of building mobile carnival games did not match the newly announced pirate theme for this year's event: Treasures of the Deep. As product owner, I helped to realign and drive the mobile app experience to be more cohesive with the event story and theme.

results

Universal Play™: Creole Carnival Games

Creole Carnival Games was a mobile app experience that entertained and rewarded guests who interacted within the Universal Orlando Resort™ app and in the real world at the Universal Orlando Mardi Gras event. The game was heavily themed to match the real-world event. The more you played, the more digital achievements (aka Treasure) you can earn!

Sponsored by The Coca-Cola® Company, Creole Carnival Games could be also accessed early by scanning the QR code found within specially marked Coca-Cola® and Coca-Cola® Zero Sugar packages in the state of Florida.

The experience drew over 11,000 players over the course of the event!





my role

Reinforcing The Brand

The Creole Carnival Games project was already in motion when I was brought in as a new product owner to lead detailed design, manage software development and oversee it through launch. I also performed tasks as UX designer.

The original pre-approved features for Creole Carnival Games consisted of 3 mobile mini-games that represented real-world carnival games: a ring toss, balloon darts, and a water-shooter. I identified that there was an inconsistency in brand message considering the event's pirate theme. I took the opportunity to advocate a re-evaluation of project goals so that we could deliver a product that was both feasible and cohesive with Mardi Gras 2020's offerings.

approach

Crafting Meaningful Stories

I led brainstorm sessions to determine a project approach. We procured data and knowledge from our Marketing Department to identify the main features of Mardi Gras as the focal point of the app experience. It was important for us to understand WHY our guests attended Mardi Gras and augment those events through our app.

audience

Our target audience was our Annual Passholder (AP) group who would be able to maximize use of their passes and be enticed to return to the event. The benefits for APs can also increase the value for other guests.


We found that the parade, concerts, and consuming the New Orleans inspired food and beverages were the top Mardi Gras attractions. We then were able to generate ideas and narrow down to the ones which were the best balance of feasible and fun!

process

Moving The Product Forward

Once we locked in our ideas, I designated tasks to each member of our team as well as our vendor. As the lead UX designer, I condensed our discussed features into a brief game design document (GDD) and distributed it to the team. I used the GDD as my guide to produce user flows, wireframes, and early mockups of the game. I also held the master copy of our game's achievement spreadsheet which the developers used for writing scripts to the game so it could be flexibly tweaked as we updated the content throughout development. We approached the project using a loose agile development methodology.

Further into development, I transitioned into approving designs as they became closer to the final product. I hosted weekly meetings with our vendor to exchange progress on the game. The vendor developed the software architecture and also produced final art and animation assets for the UI in collaboration with my team's lead artist. I communicated this progress to my stakeholders regularly for approvals.

I collaborated with different divisions of Universal Orlando Resort™ to realize to interactive features of our game such as working with the Food & Beverage group to enable QR coded receipts for tracking progress on the 'Cajun Passport' feature. Some of the other divisions I worked with included Marketing, Entertainment, Legal, and of course our Corporate Partnerships group who help to liaison with The Coca-Cola® Company.

    • impact

    Growing the Universal Play™ Platform

    This was a significant product for growing Universal Play™ as the sole permanent title on the app, the Fast & Furious Supercharged Network, did not gain much traction. The more successful Halloween Horror Nights (HHN) app experience was also a temporary experience.

    Creole Carnival Games was developed in a short time span with a less marketable timeframe, but produced a relatively well received experience compared to its HHN counterpart. Though also a temporary product, it adds Universal Play™ engagement to another 1/6th of the calendar year.

    By leveraging the backend of our existing titles, we proved that we have some solid games with the potential of becoming permanent titles for Universal Play™ while we also created some new features in parallel. This adds to the goal of establishing a whole new theme park experience that guests can enjoy using mobile.
