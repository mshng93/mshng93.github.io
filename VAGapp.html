Vancouver Art Gallery Tablet App

Interface Design



Vancouver Art Gallery Tablet App

Team: Robbie Sebullen, Kimberly Yip
Role: Interface Design, Research, User Testing
Tools: Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Axure, Moqups Online

The Gallery App allows guests to navigate within the Vancouver Art Gallery and be up to date with exhibits and tours. The app is meant to be a service that the gallery can provide for users and can also be accessed through one's own tablet.




MY ROLE

I was the interface designer in charge of organizing graphic design elements and compiling them into specific screens for the final app. The entire team participated in sketching and wireframing before we agreed on a general design. I also visited the site to conduct research on the space and its users. Finally, I conducted user tests using the Think Aloud method which were recorded and used for making design decisions.






INSIGHTS

The Vancouver Art Gallery is a dynamic environment - there are no permanent exhibits. The physical space also changes as the exhibits come and go. Thus, new and frequent guests to the gallery may be unfamiliar with the space and the staff will constantly be required to update gallery information. By introducing the Gallery App, Vancouver Art Gallery guests can quickly become more familiar with the space and gain a personal experience with the information they need at the palm of their hands.


WIREFRAMES

Initially, the interface was designed using a landscape orientation. We believed the portrait style orientation would be ergonomically easier to carry around the Art Gallery as opposed to the typical landscape orientation for maps.




USER TESTING

User testing was performed by creating different predetermined scenarios for users to run though. I facilitated several of the user tests using the Think Aloud method and a post-test questionnaire. Testing was captured by video to aid design iteration.




REFLECTIONS

Even when the interface was believed to be quite user friendly, user testing can prove things to be quite different. Performing different user tests revealed what us as designers overlooked. Redesigning is also often a compromise. We made a major change from the typical landscape orientation for using maps to a portrait orientation to accompany user interactions while holding onto the tablet during travel within the art gallery.
