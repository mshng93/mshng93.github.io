Universal Creative Tools

Designer


Confidentiality Notice: I am unable to disclose certain aspects of this project as it serves many products that are still under development.

BRIEF

Several Universal Creative projects require an ample amount of test and adjust to tune experiences for our varied theme park guests. Rather than hard-coding changes during development, I teamed up with a fellow co-worker to produce internal tools to allow for real-time test and adjust of several larger interactive experiences.

GOALS

  • Provide real-time test and adjust capabilities to the attraction/experience designers and developers.
  • Provide ability to save and load presets of desired settings for A/B testing purposes.
  • Save on future development resources by building a robust testing architecture.
  • Create templates for future experiences that could benefit from a test and adjust tool.

    • PRODUCT

    The end product provided to Project Teams is an interface that is linked to the software driving different aspects of the attraction or experience such as graphics, lighting, gaming parameters and more.

    DESIGN

    I collaborated with designers, engineers, and developers on identifying their needs and streamlining the information. Some products have specific requirements that need more custom work such as being able to save and load presets. I also tend to theme the tool to the particular intellectual property involved so it can be used in more professional demonstrations.

    Iterating on the user experience for these tools, although only used internally, was very important. The tool needed to be intuitive so different Team Members could build confidence with the tool, train other members efficiently, and deliver consistent product demonstrations. This also applied when the tool needed to also be used by vendors. Eventually, when the end-product is launched publicly, the tool could be used for continued fine-tuning after receiving real guest data.

    My final deliveries included documentation and a sprite sheet or blank feature assets that the ongoing developers could use if a new ask came up after my initial work was completed.

    REFLECTION

    Creating something for guest consumption in the theme park is the ultimate dream at Universal Parks & Resorts, but I found it just as rewarding to provide for my fellow peers, making their jobs easier or helping them to create better products. I learned that a sign of successful design is where you are able to let go and it can be self-sufficient or help to empower its new owners.
