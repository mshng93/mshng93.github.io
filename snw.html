Challenge

How do we show guests their player stats as they play in Super Nintendo World™?

Solution

A mobile application paired with guest's Power Up Band will display player activity.

Impact

We hope Super Nintendo World™ will make guests feel like they are truly in a video game unlike the world has seen before.


Context

What is Super Nintendo World™?

Super Nintendo World™ is a life-sized living video game with a whole new level of immersive play that is unlike any other theme park! This is possible with the new wearable 'Power Up Band' that is linked to guest's smartphone, allowing them to connect to elements in the land in a seamless way. The app will keep track of items collected in the land such as coins and stamps similar to items collected in Nintendo® video games.

A concept screen capture of Super Nintendo World™ where you are the player in a Nintendo game!

goals

1. Create a mobile experience to display guest's game achievements

Tracking and displaying real-time game play in a theme park land has never been done at this scale before. This experience is to only show game achievements and not personally identifiable information. The experience should also align to the Nintendo aesthetic.

The Super Nintendo World™ App Experience and Power-Up Bands.

my role

Mobile UX Design

I was involved in the concept and design of the smartphone app players will be able to use throughout the parks, including creating user flow diagrams, wireframes, early mockups, providing design feedback, test and adjust. Prior to the Global Kick-off Presentation, I helped to demonstrate the work in progress to key stakeholders.

I frequented our software debug tool to award and test various player achievements to see how they appeared in the app while the hardware for the Power Up Bands were still being developed. Once I was able to collaborate with our hardware engineering teams, I helped to test the interactions between the Power Up Bands, the show set interactives, and the companion app.

For example, you can punch Question Blocks just like Mario in Super Nintendo World™ and receive coins that will display in the app!

Project Management

I worked on establishing my team's software vendor contract for the Super Nintendo World™ app experience and also coordinated efforts between our software and hardware vendor teams. After our vertical slice was established, I transitioned from a designer role to project management leading weekly meetings and communicating with various divisions to ensure that blockers were removed so design and development could progress.

I coordinated efforts across 3 timezones including Pacific Standard Time (PST), Eastern Standard Time (EST), and Japan Standard Time (JST) with companies from countries including the United States, Canada, and Japan.
