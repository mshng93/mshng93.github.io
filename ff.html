Fast & Furious Minigames

Join the F&F Family, With the Family

ROLE

UX Design

TEAM

1-5 people

LOCATION

Orlando, FL

Brief

Design and develop a tablet game themed to the Fast & Furious franchise to be implemented in the Fast & Furious: Supercharged attraction Childswap Waiting Room.

YEAR

2017

The Challenge

Universal Orlando Resort™ Childswap Rooms are attraction waiting rooms and holding areas for families with small children, those who are unable to ride or who do not want to ride to wait for their party without needing to exit the queue. Besides seating and looping videos, Childswap Rooms do not have much else to offer for those who most frequently occupy this space.

results

They're Kids

The interactive team created 3 vehicle-themed childrens tablet minigames in parallel with the primary project team that implemented the racing slot cars. The minigames (or potentially just the tablets...) were played with often enough; let say we had to replace the iPads more than once. The games in the Fast & Furious: Supercharged Childswap Room are the first of its kind to be implemented at Universal Orlando Resort™.



approach

Make It Useful, Make It Fun

Fast & Furious is all about cars and family. As theme park designers, we strived to stay true to the intellectual property by creating vehicle-themed games suitable for the younger age demographic and their guardians. Of the top 3 guest-voiced topics desired for the theme park, this room already had 2 - seating and air conditioning. The final topic of 'more immersive environments' was where we were experts of. Often restless after waiting in long queues all day, we believed that simple, quick satisfaction games with a small learning curve would bring the most joy to those occupying this particular space.

process

Designing for Children

I brainstormed digital tablet games with my team using some initial game research and tossing our ideas onto whiteboards, grouping similarities, and making rankings. With senior designers as our advisors, we narrowed down the ideas into 4 concepts that were also on trend with popular existing childrens apps. We targeted the 3-5 year old range.

The initial concepts accepted were a coloring book, puzzle, tile matching, and Simon Says. However, the Simon Says concept was veto'd out in favor of polishing the other 3 more well-known games. We also had to consider schedule and budget constraints. Details of each game were written out and reviewed before entering production.

UX Design

I collaborated closely with the artist and developer to create the UX of the proposed tablet mini-games. We followed design specifications for our target group such as leveraging iconography, no scrolling, and using minimal text. I worked closely with the artist to design layouts and choosing graphics for the games including iterating on the interactive element sizes and navigation.

One of the biggest items we had to work through was the coloring game as we needed to use assets provided by the project team (ultimately from the Fast & Furious franchise). These car outlines were very detailed and tough to fully color in even as an adult. We worked to find the breaks that made sense in the outlined drawings to be able to successfully color in each of the cars completely even when tapping at random.

Other design considerations:

  • Homescreen icons were animated to show intended interaction
  • Font no smaller than 14-pt if used
  • Larger buttons and hit-boxes
  • Game resets after pre-determined idle period
  • Limiting choices to streamline arriving at gameplay
  • Maximizing gameplay display area vs. navigation
  • BGM vs interaction SFX

    impact

    More Inclusive, More Story

    Childswap Rooms are often overlooked or rank low in priority when a project team designs an attraction. I was delighted to join this project in particular to advocate for a more inclusive experience for those who may not have the ability to access particular attractions.

    My teammates and I designed with a long-term plan in mind - each of the games were developed and documented in hopes to transform the suite we created into a platform that coud be leveraged for future Childswap Room games. The idea was proposed with the possibility of expansion. I hope that the success of this product can help raise overall guest satisfaction and could be applied to other Childswap Rooms in the future.
